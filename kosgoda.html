KOSGODA HATCHERY

About the hatchery

In 1981, Dudley Perera launched the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project. The project's primary objective is the preservation of sea turtles and their protection against raptors like birds, dogs, and crabs. One of Sri Lanka's 18 hatcheries, it is committed to the proper reproduction of this critically endangered species. Out of the seven different species of margin turtles in the world, visitors can view up to five endangered kinds here. Although the location is most suited for children, it offers everyone a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Their work

In order to ensure that any mother turtles have suitable conditions to lay eggs, the volunteers patrol the beach at night. They also transport the eggs back to the hatchery to ensure proper hatching without predator injury, and once fully hatched, they release the young sea turtles back into the ocean. They also care for disabled sea turtles and treat those that have grown frail as a result of fishing activity.

Activities

When visiting the hatchery, visitors will receive instructions on how to safeguard and properly hatch sea turtles. It is also possible to visit the hatchery in the evening when the baby turtles are released into the ocean because there is less of a risk from predators at this time. Visitors will also get the chance to release their own baby sea turtle into the ocean.

Hours: 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.

Turtle releases at 6:30 p.m.

Turtle Haven

Turtle Haven is an organization commited to managing the turtle hatcheries of Sri Lanka. We monitor the standards and practices so as to keep a safe haven for the endagered turtles. Through us, you have the ability to get educated, donate, purchase merch done by local hatcheries and even book trips to the hatcheries.
