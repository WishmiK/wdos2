HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY

Location

Situated in Peraliya, Telwatta, Hikkaduwa which is about 2km north of the Hikkaduwa town is the Hikkaduwa Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre.

Purpose

It's a private research facility that demonstrates the turtles' progressive growth and works to preserve marine turtles, which are in risk of extinction. They are trying to educate both locals and tourists while also repopulating the area. The sand mounds where turtles lay their eggs on the beach are closely monitored to observe the hatching process.

Visting Times

Open from 9.00am - 6.00pm.
April - July are the best months to visit the turtles.

Hatchery's Work

When the young turtles are ready, they are eventually released back into the water after the turtle eggs have been carefully checked upon. Veterinarians monitor the turtles that are hurt, mostly wounded or be missing a fin due to boat engines or nets cast by fishermen. Such turtles undergo rehabilitation, before releasing into the ocean

Activities you can do

  • You can get the chance to release newborn turtles into the ocean.
  • you can learn the importance of sea turtles and theri struggle for survival.
  • Ability to watch and also feed the turtles

Turtle Haven

Turtle Haven is an organization commited to managing the turtle hatcheries of Sri Lanka. We monitor the standards and practices so as to keep a safe haven for the endagered turtles. Through us, you have the ability to get educated, donate, purchase merch done by local hatcheries and even book trips to the hatcheries.
